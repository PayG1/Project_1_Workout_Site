Health benefits when you work out your chest?



  • Posture: As one of the largest muscles in your upper body, the pecs play a major role in maintaining good posture. In addition to your back and shoulder muscles, your pecs help to stabilize the shoulder joint.

  • Better Breathing: In addition to improving your posture, strengthening and lengthening chest muscles helps to support deeper breathing. That`s because the pecs are attached to your ribs, which expand with every breath.

  • Breast Support: Contrary to the popular belief that doing chest exercises will make breasts smaller, doing pectoral exercises will actually do the exact opposite. By building muscle around breast tissue, this helps to provide more lift and support.

  • Everyday Perks: Even if you don`t realize it, you use your pecs an awful lot during daily activities. Anything that involves lifting, holding, squeezing or pushing calls on your pecs; so it doesn`t hurt to have a little extra strength in that muscle group.
